1/8 “Dangerous” should not mean aggressive. It should mean capable: calm enough to keep constructive options under pressure.

2/8 Most people train the appearance of discipline—quotes, routines, posture—before they train reliable action.

3/8 Automatic comfort is a trap. Friction appears; a feed, snack, or delay removes it before a deliberate choice can happen.

4/8 Borrowed identity is another. Consuming another person’s discipline starts to feel like doing the work. It is not.

5/8 Real Stoicism is not numbness. Notice anger, fear, and pride. Then choose the action that serves the objective.

6/8 Seven drills: safe physical friction, silence, one clear truth, a room read, a delayed reply, one hard promise, one controlled exit.

7/8 Miss a day? Record why, resume, and redesign the environment. Punishment is not a production system.

8/8 Pressure test: if the next hour turned difficult, would you have constructive options or familiar excuses? The next repetition belongs to you.
